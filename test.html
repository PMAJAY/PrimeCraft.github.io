How analogue sound can be represented in digital form

Before we start you need to know the difference between analogue and digital sound. Analogue sound is continuous signal of vibrations that move the air molecules and transmit a continuous acoustic wave. Digital sound is where we use technology to record, store and alter analogue signals into digital form.

Once we havecaptured sounds using a microphone we can draw a graph of a soundwave and split it up into smaller, regular time intervals to become a series of samples. This will allow us to measure the amplitudes and obtain a sample rate that can be converted to binary and during the quantisation stage by mapping these samples to a band and giving that band a number.
